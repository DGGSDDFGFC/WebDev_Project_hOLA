Car of the week: F1 McLaren

The McLaren F1 was the ultimate street car of the Twentieth Century. That was the consensus of the motoring press, and certainly McLaren’s intent. Who better to unseat Ferrari, Lamborghini, Bugatti and Jaguar than the factory that had accumulated five consecutive Group 7 (Can Am) world championships, three Indy 500 victories and six Formula 1 titles?

Car of the previous week: Porshe 911

The Porsche 911 was the definitive sports car of the Twentieth Century. That was the consensus of enthusiasts and critics alike — and undoubtedly Porsche’s mission from the start. Who better to define the perfect balance of performance, engineering, and everyday usability than the marque that dominated endurance racing with countless Le Mans victories, world rally championships, and decades of success in virtually every motorsport discipline?
